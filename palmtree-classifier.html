Classifying Palmtrees

After successfully finding images similar in colour, it becomes obvious that not only colour but also the content of an image plays a role in how aesthetical we perceive a combination of images.
We will do this by splitting the process into several steps:

Data Collection

Taking my set of favourite pictures, only around 10% contain palmtrees. To avoid imbalanced training data, I scrape and clean the top palmtree images from my favourite photo sharing social media platform. This increases the percentage of palmtree images to 30%. To increase the amount further, data augmentation is another good option.

Data Pre-processing

The images are annotated with the right label. In the next step, images are croped and downsized to 100x100 pixels. The order of downsizing and annotating does play a role since humans are nearly Bayes classifiers on larger images but not anymore when it comes to very small images.

Training different Models

For estimating how heavy of a model is needed, several algorithms are tested. The performance metric is accuracy.

  • Logistic Regression with Numpy
  • Classic Neural Network with Numpy
  • Convolutional Neural Network with Tensorflow
  • Residual Neural Network with Tensorflow
  • Pre-Trained with Tensorflow
